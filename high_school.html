Edina High School


Civil Engineering & Architecture

Our Civil Engineering Architecture class was my introduction into the built environment. I really enjoyed working with Revit and Sketchup. I have created a "Practical" Dream House and a Balsa Wood Shed as the primary pieces of work from the course.

Learnings

Our Civil Engineering Architecture class assignment was to create a “practical” dream house. My intent was to design an open-concept, simplistic layout with modern furnishings. I enjoyed thinking about how to transform the space to make it functional and livable. The contrast of glass and wood textures is a primary focus of my modern exterior.

Pictured above is also an engineering focused project done in my Civil Engineering Architecture course. The end product turned into a balsa wood model of the simple shed we diagrammed on Revit. It is important because it displayed my ability to work with my hands.

Columbia Summer Immersion

I also attended a three-week summer immersion program at Columbia University in New York City to explore the field of architecture further. Here, I worked on architectural hand drawings and created my version of Parc de la Villette off the High Line.

Learnings

To begin the Columbia University summer program, we used postcards to create our own 3D model and made perspective drawings of them.

My final project for the Columbia program. The High Line is to the left of the rap battle arena. The stage is shaped like a tree trunk, the branches are suites, shaped as either, squares or triangles. Nature was a heavy influence in the postmodernist design of this space and was used to blend the High Line together with my design.
